Help! I’m completely out of water! What can I do to get it back on?

First thing you can do, before you call anyone, is to reset the breaker. Even if it doesn’t looked tripped, it can be disengaged enough to break the circuit. If you don’t know which breaker is it, you can simply reset them all (as well as your clocks).

If the breaker isn’t at fault, you should call us so we can walk you through a couple other possibilities. However, we do not encourage you to open electrical boxes on your own and tinker with the parts. The risk of electrocution is too great if you are unfamiliar or uncomfortable with them.

I’m considering a filtration system, but I’m not sure I need it. How can I be sure?

There are four major facets of water quality that you should evaluate before buying a system from anyone: Color, Smell, Taste, and Contamination.

To test for contamination, simply contact the health department for the county you live in and request a Bacteriological test (there will be a small fee). This test looks for the presence of bacteria, specifically coliform bacteria (it is sometimes called a coliform test). Wells that test positive should have a chlorination system installed to prevent illness. Other tests for lead and other chemicals are also available, for additional peace of mind.

Testing for color, smell and taste is much easier. Simply pour your water in a large, clean and clear glass container and evaluate it using your senses. Place a sheet of blank computer paper behind the glass to evaluate the color, and use hot water when checking for odor (hot water amplifies odor). Look for sediment that falls to the bottom of the glass, and check your fixtures for scale or rust stains. Then if you still aren’t sure...

What kinds of tests are there to evaluate water, and can I trust the results?

Mosts tests and kits are extremely reliable and simple, especially if you are doing it yourself and can duplicate the results over two or three testings. However, there have been reports in the past of companies employing methods that are misleading, especially when a sale depends on it. Nowadays, there is little reason for a homeowner or business owner to rely on a salesman to do their water testing, because test kits are cheap and readily available on the internet. We use a very simple kit that evaluates PH, Hardness and Dissolved Iron in minutes. It is nearly identical to a test strip kit you would use to check a pool for PH and chlorine content.

Why is there a charge for travel on my invoice?

Most companies that provide on the road services do charge separately for travel. Those that don’t charge for travel separately are making it up somewhere else in their pricing structure, usually with an inflated service charge or hourly rate. Our clients tend to side with us, preferring transparency in our billing. Plus, if your neighbor becomes a client on your route, we adjust your travel charge down in kind, resulting in a visible discount on every bill.
